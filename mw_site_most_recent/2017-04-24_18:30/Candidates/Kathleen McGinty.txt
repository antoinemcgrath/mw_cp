|WikiDraftSum=Katie McGinty was Congressional Staff Coordinator for the Senate delegation to the 1992 U.N. Conference on Environment and Development (the Rio Summit) and was an Official Member of the U.S. Delegations to Negotiations on the Framework Convention on Climate Change and the Antarctic Protocol. She was Chair of President Clinton's Council on Environmental Quality (CEQ) from 1995-98, serving as his senior advisor on environment, natural resources and sustainable development issues (1). In 2015 she stated, “the issue of climate change presents a serious global threat to our health, economic well-being and national security… I urge our leaders to continue to lead the way to a healthier and safer environment by agreeing to commonsense climate protections with investments in energy efficiency and clean energy… Experts agree that in order to avoid the worst impacts of climate change, we need to dramatically reduce climate pollution globally” (2). On her Campaign Issues page, McGinty states “Climate change presents a serious global threat to our health, economic well-being and national security. In the Senate, I will lead the way to a healthier and safer environment by working to pass commonsense climate protections with investments in energy efficiency and clean energy” (3). On April 22, 2016 she said “in the Senate, it will be my privilege to be a voice for commonsense environmental protections, and to lead the fight against climate change while investing in a resilient and inclusive clean energy economy” (4).
